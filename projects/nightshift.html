Nightshift at B-Mart

Nightshift at B-Mart was a group project completed for my Year 2 Vertical Slice module. I designed and programmed the game alongside a teammate, and managed Github source control for our team of 12; six Games Design students, five Animation students, and a Sound Design student.

Initially conceived as a Papers, Please-like puzzle sim, we quickly iterated on our idea to create Nightshift at B-Mart, an eerie shop management sim for Oculus Quest 2.





N@BM had three NPC types with varying behaviours: typical customers, shoplifters, and a unique fugitive. I explored a few different options for creating their behaviour, including UE5's experimental StateTrees and a custom FSM, but I decided to stick with Behaviour Trees; our various NPCs only have a few distinct states that are typically progessed through linearly, so there was no need for a complex network of states and transitions.

As the player is confined to their checkout counter, we devised a CCTV system mechanic to let the player monitor other areas of the store, taking some inspiration from Five Nights at Freddy's.
